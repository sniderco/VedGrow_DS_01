# COVID-19 Global Data Analysis
## Insight Report

### Objective

The objective of this analysis was to explore global COVID-19 trends and identify patterns related to confirmed cases, mortality, recovery, and disease spread across countries and WHO regions.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. United States was the most affected country

The United States recorded the highest number of confirmed COVID-19 cases among all countries analyzed, significantly exceeding other nations.

### 2. Brazil and India experienced substantial outbreaks

Brazil and India emerged as the second and third most affected countries respectively, indicating widespread transmission during the analyzed period.

### 3. Recovery performance varied across countries

Several countries demonstrated recovery rates exceeding 90%, suggesting effective treatment strategies and healthcare responses.

### 4. High case count does not necessarily imply high mortality

Countries with large numbers of confirmed cases did not always exhibit the highest death rates, indicating that healthcare capacity and intervention strategies play a significant role.

### 5. Eastern Mediterranean region showed significant mortality variation

Yemen recorded an exceptionally high death rate compared with neighboring countries, highlighting disparities in healthcare infrastructure and pandemic response.

### 6. Americas displayed high spread rates

Several countries in the Americas region recorded spread rates above 30%, indicating rapid transmission dynamics.

### 7. WHO regional differences were evident

Distinct patterns emerged across WHO regions regarding mortality and recovery rates, reflecting differences in public health policies, healthcare systems, and demographic factors.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that COVID-19 affected countries differently in terms of infection rates, mortality, recovery, and spread. Regional healthcare capacity, public health measures, and demographic characteristics likely contributed to these variations.

The project highlights the importance of data analytics and visualization in understanding global health crises and supporting evidence-based decision-making.
